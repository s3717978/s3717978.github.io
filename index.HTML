Hi, I'm Alex Bobby

Student at RMIT, Bachelor of Information Technology

Born in 1999 in a rural part of India. I had limited exposure to technology and my first use of a computer was at the age of 8. As most kids the only thing used it for was playing games, but I was always intrigued by how it works. We moved to Australia as at the age of 11 and my view of technology changed completely, the complexity of these tiny machines and the vast power they had. Even after moving we choose to speak our mother tongue in our house and being brought up in India I was able to learn another language so all together I am able to speak 3 languages. I got hands on my first iPad during my year 7 years, an iPad 3 which I still have today, somewhere. As well as the love to technology I am a car person too. my love towards cars grew as I felt the speed that could be had within them. The noise these wonderful machines make and the personality it has always attracted me and one of my favourites is the Nissan GT-R R34 V Spec II. The very top of my to-do list would be to travel the world with someone and explore what different countries have to give.

My interest in IT started at a very young age. When our family bought a computer in India there weren’t many other who had one, so I felt like having something special. Even though I only used it to play games I kept wanting to know more about how it works and what goes on inside. Later on, after gaining a bit of knowledge about computers I was the ‘tech’ guy in our family and everyone would come to me for any help and I liked fixing stuff for them, gave me a satisfaction of actually doing something. Having that authority was something special for me so therefor I wanted to know more about it hence why I chose to study IT.
